# How To Talk so Kids Will Listen and Listen so Kids will Talk - Adlele Faber & Elaine Mazlish

## 1. Helping Children Deal with Their Feelings
- Children need to have their feelings accepted and respected.
- To help with feelings
  - You can listen quitely and attentively
  - You can acknowledge their feelings with a word. eg: "Oh...mm.. I see.."
  - You can give he feeling a name. eg: "That sounds frustrating"
  - You can give the child his whishes in fantasy.
- What people of all ages can use in a moment of distress is not agreeemnt or disagreement; they need someone to recognize what it is they're experiencing.
## 2. Engaging Cooperation
- To engage a child's copoperation
  - Describe what you see, or describe the problem. eg: " there's a wet towel on the bed"
  - Give information. eg: "The towel is getting my blanket wet"
  - Say it with a word. eg: "The towel"
  - Describe what you feel. eg: "I don't like sleeping in a wet bed!"
  - Write a note. eg: (Above towel rack) Please put me back here - Thanks, Your Towel
